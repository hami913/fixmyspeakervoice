Sound and Vibration: How They Work Together

It is necessary to understand that sound (a form of energy made by vibrations) and vibration(To and fro or back and forth motion of an object or particles in a medium) have different roles. But in Fix, my speaker, these two things work together, proving beneficial. We explain sound and vibration in two different terms to make you understand. I will also tell you how they prove to be beneficiaries together.

Sound Waves:

Sound waves are longitudinal waves that travel through a medium like air or water. Playing a low-frequency sound, like a 165Hz tone(ideal), through your speaker creates sound waves that make the speaker’s diaphragm. (The thin, semi-rigid membrane attached to the voice coil) move back and forth. This movement creates vibrations inside the speaker, which help shake loose any water or dust blocking the sound.

Vibrations:

When an object vibrates, it generates movement, which is strong enough to dislodge and expel water droplets, dust, or small particles from the speaker. As a result, these blockages are cleared, allowing the sound to reemerge and enhancing the speaker’s performance.

NOTE: In simple terms, sound waves create vibrations, which help clear out anything stopping your speaker from working correctly.

Common Ways Water and Dust Enter Your Phone:

Sometimes, water and dust can get into your phone speaker directly or indirectly through various ways, such as Humidity, Dirty pockets or bags, and Outdoor activities (rain, snow, sand). Using the phone while cooking, Sweat, Inadequate protection (poor cases), Frequent handling with dirty hands, etc. Due to this, they create various technical issues in the mobile, distorting your phone speaker’s quality and functionalities. Here’s an expanded explanation of the problems caused by water and dust in your phone, along with some additional points:

Shortened Battery Life or Overheating

 Moisture can cause short circuits in the phone, which makes it use more power to work correctly. This can lead to the phone overheating and a significant drop in battery life by about 30% to 70%, sometimes causing it to shut down suddenly.

Reduced Volume or Clarity

 When water or dust blocks the speaker, the sound can become quieter by about 30% to 70%. This can make it hard to hear calls, notifications, or music clearly, and the sound quality can be frustrating during phone calls or while listening to music.

Muffled or Distorted Sound:

Water or dust entering the speaker can block the sound waves. This blockage muffles the audio, meaning the voices and music sound unclear and less lively.

Corrosion or Damage to Speaker

Being exposed to moisture for a long time can cause dust on the inside parts of the speaker. This damage can impact the speaker and other essential parts of the phone, which may result in expensive repairs or replacements.

Software Issues

Sometimes, when a phone gets wet, it can cause software problems, like audio apps or settings acting strangely. This can make using the phone more difficult.

Audio Feedback or Echo

Dust and water can mess up the phone’s microphones, causing sound problems like feedback or echo during calls. This can make it complicated and annoying for both people to talk.

Signal Interference

Moisture and dirt can also damage the phone’s antennae, resulting in weak signal reception and connection problems. This affects both calls and data services.

Physical Damage

Water or dust getting into the phone can cause physical damage over time, like problems with the screen or buttons not working correctly. This can affect how well the phone functions overall. In the case of the iPhone, it can affect their face ID.

By knowing about these possible problems, you can take steps to keep your phone safe from water and dust damage, which helps it work better and last longer.

Buy a reliable waterproof case for your phone. It will keep the speakers safe from water or dust. You can use your phone without worrying about spills with a good case. A strong case helps protect and clear the speaker of your phone every day.

Regular Cleaning: 

Clean the speaker holes regularly using a soft brush or a microfiber cloth every two weeks. This prevents dust from piling up and helps keep the sound clear. Even a small amount of cleaning can help your speakers work better. Try to make it a habit to check for dirt often.

Silica Gel Packs: 

Keep silica gel(to keep things dry) packs close to your phone to soak up moisture. These packs help lower humidity around the device. With less moisture, there’s a lower risk of water damage. You can usually find these packs in shoe boxes or packaging for electronics.

Avoid Using Phone in Wet Areas: 

People often use phones in bathrooms to relax and listen to music and in swimming pools to take pics and snaps and capture their moments. Avoid using your phone in bathrooms or near swimming pools. This helps stop accidental spills or getting it wet. Keeping your phone dry is crucial for the speakers. Look for a safe place away from water when you use your phone.

Limit Outdoor Use in Dusty Conditions:

 When it is dusty outside, try not to use your phone without a case. Dust can quickly enter the speaker and create issues. Keeping your phone protected during outdoor activities helps it stay cleaner. A case can keep dust and dirt from getting inside.

Play Sound at Lower Volumes: 

Keep the volume reasonable when listening to music or making calls. This reduces vibrations that can stir up dirt. Lower volumes can help stop dust from entering the speakers. It also improves the overall sound quality.

Dry Off After Exposure: 

If your phone gets wet, dry it off immediately with a soft cloth and (disinfectant wipes with 70% isopropyl alcohol). This helps keep water from entering the speakers. Acting quickly can prevent damage and keep your phone running smoothly. Always check for moisture after using it in wet places.

Avoid Eating or Drinking While Using Your Phone:

Avoid eating or drinking while using your phone. This simple habit can help stop spills that might harm the speakers. Keeping food and drinks away makes your phone safer and your physical and mental well-being. It also helps keep your phone clean and working well.

Use Speaker Covers:

Regular Software Updates:

Other Methods Rather Than Using Sound

Sometimes, using sound may not work very well, especially if water or debris is stuck deeply inside or if a lot of moisture is present. So, there are some general steps for removing water from a mobile speaker.

GENERAL STEPS

  • Turn Off the Phone: First, turn off your phone to avoid short circuits.
  • Remove Accessories: Take off any cases of screen protectors, and unplug all cables.
  • Dry the Outside: Use a soft cloth to wipe down the phone, especially the speaker area.
  • Shake Gently: Tilt the phone and gently shake it to drain any extra water from the speaker holes.
  • Use a Vacuum: If you have one, use a vacuum cleaner (without any attachments) to suck out moisture from the speaker area. Keep it a few inches away to prevent damage.

For iPhone:

  • Don’t Charge It: Avoid charging your phone until it is scorched.
  • Put It in a Dry Place: Place your iPhone in a dry area with good airflow, like near a fan. Please stay away from direct sunlight, as it can overheat the phone.
  • Wait: Leave the phone for at least 24-48 hours to ensure it dries completely.

For Android:

  • Remove SIM and SD Cards: Remove the SIM(Subscriber Identity Module) and SD(secure digital) cards to help them dry and prevent rust.
  • Use Rice or Silica Gel: Place your phone in a bowl of uncooked rice or with silica gel packets. These help absorb moisture.
  • Avoid Heat: Don’t use a hairdryer or place your phone near heaters, which can cause more damage.

After Care:

  • Check for Problems: After waiting a day or two, turn on your phone and check if the speaker works well.
  • Seek Help if Needed: If you notice any sound issues or water damage continues, get help from a professional technician.

Following these steps can help reduce water damage and increase the chances of your phone’s speaker working correctly again. If you have more concerns, contact us.
